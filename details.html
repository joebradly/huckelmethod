    Hückel method is a simple web utility, which finds the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions for simple Hückel models, and presents them graphically.

    Hückel models are simple chemistry models for the pi-system of molecules, which completely ignore electron-electron interactions. In physics lingo, a Hückel model is a non-interacting tight-binding model, and for mathematicians a Hückel model is an undirected graph where nodes and links can have unequal weights.

    The Hückel method diagonalizes this matrix and presents the eigenfunctions graphically. Because the weights of all links and nodes are real, the eigenfunctions and eigenvalues are also real. For Hückel molecules the eigenfunctions are also known as the molecular orbitals, while the eigenvalues are the eigenenergies.

    Wikipedia also have some articles about Hückel theory and spectral graph theory.

    The Hückel method solver is built to be flexible. If you instead prefer a more chemistry oriented application, where you can choose hetero-atoms, and which shows all auxiliary atoms, you could try out the free hülis program.
